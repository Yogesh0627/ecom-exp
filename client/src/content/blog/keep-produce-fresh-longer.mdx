Roughly a third of the fresh produce a household buys ends up in the bin. That's not carelessness —
it's mostly storing things the wrong way. A handful of rules will noticeably cut what you throw out,
and the money that goes with it.

## The one word that explains most of it: ethylene

Some fruits release **ethylene**, a ripening gas. Others are highly *sensitive* to it and spoil fast
in its presence. Keep the two groups apart and everything lasts longer.

- **Ethylene producers:** bananas, apples, tomatoes, avocados, mangoes.
- **Ethylene sensitive:** leafy greens, broccoli, cucumbers, carrots.

That lone banana is why your spinach wilted. Store the two on opposite sides of the kitchen.

## Fridge or counter?

**Keep on the counter** (cold ruins texture or flavour):
- Tomatoes — refrigeration kills the taste and turns them mealy.
- Bananas, mangoes, and other tropical fruit until ripe.
- Onions, garlic, potatoes — cool, dark, dry, and *never* next to each other (potatoes make onions
  sprout).

**Keep in the fridge:**
- Leafy greens and herbs — wrapped in a dry cloth to absorb moisture.
- Berries — but don't wash until you eat them; surface water breeds mould.
- Carrots, broccoli, cauliflower.

## Small habits, big savings

1. **Don't wash before storing.** Moisture accelerates rot. Wash right before eating.
2. **Revive limp greens** in a bowl of cold water for 15 minutes instead of tossing them.
3. **Freeze the about-to-turn.** Overripe bananas, spare herbs in oil, chopped vegetables past their
   prime — the freezer is a pause button, not a graveyard.
4. **Shop your fridge first.** Most waste is buying more of what you already have, forgotten at the
   back of a shelf.

## Where a smart kitchen helps

Knowing what you already own is half the battle. EcoExpress's **pantry** and **smart fridge scanner**
keep a running picture of what's in your kitchen and what's about to expire — so the meal planner
can suggest using the spinach *before* it wilts, not after.

Buy well, store smart, and the weekly produce bin gets a lot emptier.
